# Kinfolk Brand Identity

**Your family's digital gathering place.**

---

## Brand Essence

### Core Values

**1. Family-First**
- Designed for togetherness
- Multi-generational usability
- Shared experiences over individual consumption

**2. Privacy-Conscious**
- Local-first processing
- No telemetry or tracking
- User data stays in user control
- Transparent about what goes to cloud

**3. Open & Honest**
- Open-source codebase
- Community-driven development
- No vendor lock-in
- Transparent roadmap

**4. Human-Centered**
- Voice-first, touch-second
- Glanceable information
- Reduces screen time, doesn't add to it
- Brings family together, not apart

---

## Brand Personality

**If Kinfolk were a person:**
- Warm and welcoming (not cold/clinical)
- Helpful without being intrusive
- Trustworthy and reliable
- Modern but not trendy
- Family-oriented but not childish

**Tone of Voice:**
- Friendly, not corporate
- Clear, not technical
- Warm, not overly casual
- Inclusive, not exclusive

---

### Color Palette

**Primary Colors:**

**Warm Clay** `#D4A574`
- Main accent color
- Earthy, warm, inviting
- Use for: Primary buttons, highlights, active states

**Deep Charcoal** `#2A2A2E`
- Background dark mode
- Professional, modern
- Use for: Backgrounds, dark surfaces

**Soft Cream** `#F5F3ED`
- Light mode background
- Warm, paper-like
- Use for: Light backgrounds, cards

**Secondary Colors:**

**Forest Green** `#4A7C59`
- Success states, nature connection
- Calming, grounding

**Sky Blue** `#7BA7BC`
- Information, links
- Trustworthy, clear

**Sunset Orange** `#E07856`
- Warnings, important alerts
- Energetic, attention-grabbing

**Sage Gray** `#9CA3A8`
- Secondary text, borders
- Neutral, balanced

---

### Typography

**Primary Font: Inter**
- Clean, readable, modern
- Excellent at all sizes
- Great for UI elements
- Free & open-source

**Secondary Font: Literata**
- Warm, slightly serif
- Use for: Quotes, featured text, welcome messages
- Adds personality without sacrificing readability

**Monospace: JetBrains Mono**
- Developer docs, code examples
- Clean, modern monospace

**Font Scale:**
```
Display:  48px / 600 weight
H1:       36px / 600 weight
H2:       28px / 600 weight
H3:       22px / 500 weight
Body:     16px / 400 weight
Small:    14px / 400 weight
Caption:  12px / 400 weight
```

---

### Iconography

**Style:**
- Rounded corners (4px radius)
- 2px stroke weight
- Filled for active states
- Outlined for inactive
- Warm, friendly aesthetic

**Icon Library:** Lucide Icons (open-source, customizable)

### Voice Persona

**Name:** "Kin" (short for Kinfolk)

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Warm, friendly tone
- Clear pronunciation
- Not overly enthusiastic or robotic
- Gender-neutral default voice
- User can customize

**Example Responses:**
```
User: "What's the weather?"
Kin: "It's 72 and sunny today, with a high of 78. Perfect weather for a family walk!"

User: "Add milk to the shopping list"
Kin: "Added milk to your shopping list. You have 4 items now."

User: "Good morning"
Kin: "Good morning, James! It's 7:30 AM. You have 2 events on your calendar today."
```

## Competitive Positioning

### vs Amazon Echo Show
- ✅ Privacy-first (no Amazon surveillance)
- ✅ No subscription required
- ✅ Open-source & customizable
- ✅ Larger display
- ❌ Less mature ecosystem (initially)

### vs Google Nest Hub
- ✅ No Google tracking
- ✅ Works without Google account
- ✅ Open-source
- ✅ Better customization
- ❌ Setup requires more tech knowledge

### vs Magic Mirror
- ✅ Better voice integration
- ✅ Family-focused features
- ✅ More polished UI
- ✅ Easier setup
- ❌ Not quite as minimal/mirror aesthetic

## Brand Guidelines

### Do's ✅

- Use warm, family-focused language
- Emphasize privacy and open-source
- Show real family use cases
- Use inclusive imagery (diverse families)
- Keep UI clean and glanceable
- Make voice interactions natural

### Don'ts ❌

- Don't use corporate/sterile language
- Don't oversell or hype
- Don't show perfect/staged families
- Don't ignore privacy concerns
- Don't make UI cluttered
- Don't make voice sound robotic

## Photography Style

**For Marketing/Docs:**
- Real families (not stock photos)
- Natural lighting
- In-home settings
- Showing actual usage (not posed)
- Diverse family structures
- Focus on connection, not technology

- Minimal backgrounds
- Warm lighting
- Show scale (with household objects)
